MASTER‑AERO‑DOC‑V17C — UNIVERSAL SCENARIO STANDARD
Universal Master Document for Scenario Construction (V16C Edition)  
Valid for all pilot competencies: KNO, RES, M&I, COM, SAW, WLM, PSD, FPA, FPM, LTM, PRO, etc.
Scenario Title and Competency
Scenario Title  
Scenario number + descriptive title.
Competency  
Full name of the competency.
Include the official competency definition.
Primary OB  
OB code + OB name.
Only one Primary OB per scenario.
No definitions.
Context
The Context must:
Describe the initial situation clearly and operationally.
Include only information needed to understand the starting point.
Establish temporal, spatial, and operational frame.
Avoid analysis, CRM, TEM, judgments, or explanations.
Be rewritten if unclear or poorly written.
Operational Expectations  
Bullet points describing what the crew is expected to do at the start.
Sequence of Events
Chronological list of events.
Each event must follow this structure:
Line 1: Event
Line 2: OB (code + name only)
Rules:
OB definitions must never appear.
OB must be on a separate line.
No parentheses.
No explanatory text.
No OB inside the event line.
No analysis.
Operational Flow & Dialogue
Full, realistic dialogue.
Each dialogue block must follow this structure:

Line 2: OB (code + name only)
Rules:
OB definitions must never appear.
OB must be on its own line.
No parentheses.
No explanations.
If speaker is not specified, deduce logically and write:
Speaker:  
“Phrase.”

Idiomatic Cockpit English Rule (MANDATORY)
Dialogue must:
Use natural, idiomatic cockpit English
Be concise and operational
Avoid robotic or textbook phrasing
Remain professional and clear
Examples allowed:
“Let’s run the abnormal.”
“Looks like a spurious.”
“Good call.”
“Let’s keep an eye on that.”
“I’ll take control.”

TEM — Threat & Error Management
(Hazards, errors, mistakes, threats and mitigations identified in this scenario, as well as those that could reasonably have occurred under similar circumstances with a different crew.)
Each category must include its definition EXACTLY as follows:
Hazards
(Events, conditions or circumstances — external or internal — that deviate or distort the normal operation of the flight and alter the planned sequence.)
Errors
(Knowledge‑based errors: incorrect actions or decisions caused by lack of knowledge, misunderstanding, or incorrect mental models.)
Mistakes
(Attention‑based errors: distraction, forgetfulness, omission, or temporary loss of attention.)
Threats
(Consequences that can cause harm to the aircraft, the crew, the passengers, or the operation, arising logically from the combination of hazards, errors and mistakes.)
Undesired Aircraft State
(The state in which the aircraft and the operation find themselves — or will find themselves — as the logical consequence of the Threats.)
Aircraft impact
(How the aircraft is physically or technically affected.)
Operational impact
(How the operation, workload, sequence or safety margin is affected.)
Mitigations
(Actions the crew took — or could reasonably have taken — to prevent reaching the Undesired Aircraft State.)
Rules:
Bullet‑based.
Must include all categories.
Must reference exact phrases from the scenario.
OBs only when tied to a phrase.
No narrative.

RES — Resilience
The capacity to overcome adversity and emerge fortified from it, through the development of productive capacity.
R.1 Prevents, minimizes, or overcomes the negative effects of a situation
R.2 Shows endurance in the face of adversity
R.3 Is continuously adapted to new developments and challenges
R.4 Is flexible
R.5 Is positive
R.6 Evaluates the specific situation in order to make the best decision
R.7 Practices reflection in the action, knowledge in action, and reflection on the action
R.8 Shows confidence in himself and in others
R.9 Manages his own stress
R.10 Diminishes stressing others
KNO — Application of Knowledge
Demonstrates knowledge and understanding of relevant information, operating instructions, aircraft systems and the operating environment.
0.1 Practical and applicable knowledge of limitations and systems
0.2 Knowledge of published operating instructions
0.3 Knowledge of physical and ATC environment
0.4 Knowledge of applicable legislation
0.5 Knows where to source required information
0.6 Positive interest in acquiring knowledge
0.7 Applies knowledge effectively
